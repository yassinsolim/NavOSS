# Code of Conduct

## Our Standards

NavOSS contributors, maintainers, and community members must help create a respectful, inclusive, and technically honest project.

Positive behavior includes:

- giving and accepting constructive, evidence-based feedback;
- respecting different experience levels, identities, and viewpoints;
- protecting personal location, identity, and safety information;
- acknowledging mistakes and correcting them clearly; and
- prioritizing user and road safety over feature velocity.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, discrimination, threats, sexualized attention, doxxing, publishing another person's location or private correspondence, deliberate misinformation, or sustained disruption of project spaces.

## Scope

This policy applies in repository discussions, pull requests, issue trackers, testing sessions, community events, and public representation of NavOSS.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit or remove content and may temporarily or permanently restrict participation when conduct threatens people or the project. Enforcement decisions should be proportionate, documented privately, and avoid exposing reporters or affected people.

Do not place a confidential conduct report in a public issue. Contact the maintainer through the private contact method listed on [@yassinsolim's GitHub profile](https://github.com/yassinsolim). A dedicated `navoss@yassin.app` forwarding address will replace this route once it is active.

Good-faith safety, privacy, or security reports will not be treated as disruption. Security vulnerabilities should use the process in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).

## Attribution
