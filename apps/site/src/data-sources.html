Provenance and limitations

Know where the map comes from

Map and place data

NavOSS uses OpenStreetMap-derived map and place data together with The City of Calgary's public Business Licenses and Parcel Address datasets. OpenStreetMap is open data licensed under the Open Data Commons Open Database License. Visible attribution names both OpenStreetMap contributors and The City of Calgary.

Calgary records are mirrored to a private local index every 24 hours; live searches do not go to Calgary Open Data. Home-occupation business records and malformed parcel rows are excluded. No source can guarantee every informal, new, relocated, or unlicensed place, and parcel coordinates may not identify the best vehicle entrance.

Routing

Development routes are calculated by Valhalla using OpenStreetMap data. The public development endpoint has no production availability guarantee and is visibly reported as degraded. A self-hosted regional service is required before beta distribution.

Safety cameras

Fixed red-light and speed-on-green locations come from The City of Calgary's Intersection Safety Cameras dataset. NavOSS validates and caches the official records without sending user location to Calgary Open Data.

Camera data can be incomplete or outdated. Alerts are supplementary and never replace posted signs or road laws.

What NavOSS does not use

NavOSS does not scrape or copy route, place, traffic, or map data from Apple Maps, Google Maps, Waze, or other proprietary navigation products. Manual comparisons are evidence for testing, not a source database.

Detailed registry

Dataset IDs, update cadence, fields, safeguards, and links are maintained in the repository's data-source documentation.
